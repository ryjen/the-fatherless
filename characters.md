

## **1. Sael – The Fatherless Child / Protagonist**

**Starting Point:**

* Innocent, fatherless by cruel design. Unaware of the manipulation behind his birth.

**Conflict:**

* Must grapple with the trauma of his origin, the expectations of the cult, and the hostility of Roman elites.
* Struggles to reconcile the desire for normal life with his symbolic role.

**Growth / Transformation:**

* Develops deep moral clarity through the teachings of the cult (Stoicism, Judaism, Eastern philosophies).
* Learns forgiveness, even for those who caused him harm.
* Becomes a moral and spiritual leader, challenging systemic corruption through example rather than force.

**Ending State:**

* Fully embraces his role as a symbol of divine morality and forgiveness.
* Inspires the cult and others, leaving a legacy that outlives his physical life.

**Themes in Arc:**

* Forgiveness as power.
* Moral authority vs. political authority.
* Humanity born from cruelty can be a force of good.

---

## **2. Cassian – Slave Master / Antagonist**

**Starting Point:**

* Ambitious, morally conflicted Roman elite. Obsessed with status, political power, and revenge.
* Engages in cruelty to maintain control and the illusion of glory.

**Conflict:**

* Feels his plans undermined by Sael’s existence and moral authority.
* Struggles with envy, hubris, and the threat to his reputation.

**Growth / Transformation:**

* Does not grow morally; instead, his arc is a **descent into humiliation**.
* His cruelty is exposed as ineffective against true moral power.
* Loses political and social standing; becomes a symbol of arrogance punished by circumstance.

**Ending State:**

* Humiliated and politically weakened.
* Represents the failure of cruelty and ego when faced with moral integrity.

**Themes in Arc:**

* Hubris vs. morality.
* Political power cannot supplant moral authority.
* Cruelty begets failure when challenged by conscience and ethics.

---

## **3. Maro – Conflicted Insider / Moral Foil**

**Starting Point:**

* Young Roman servant; participates in elite indulgences.
* Envious of Sael’s “privilege” in birth and opportunity.

**Conflict:**

* Torn between loyalty to elite class and recognition of moral injustice.
* Fears punishment for confessing truth about Sael.

**Growth / Transformation:**

* Confesses knowledge to the cult, seeking redemption.
* Becomes a guide and link between Sael and Roman society.
* Learns humility and responsibility for his envy.

**Ending State:**

* Redeemed morally, becomes a bridge between corrupt society and moral authority.
* Shows that conscience and courage can overcome personal weakness.

**Themes in Arc:**

* Guilt, redemption, and moral courage.
* Personal envy transformed into ethical action.
* One person’s confession can catalyze great change.

---

## **4. Sael’s Parents – Innocent Victims / Symbols**

**Starting Point:**

* Simple slaves, unaware of Cassian’s manipulation.
* Oblivious to their own relationship that leads to Sael’s birth.

**Conflict:**

* Confronted with the revelation of their child’s conception.
* Must trust others to raise their child in a dangerous and morally complex world.

**Growth / Transformation:**

* Accept Sael’s fate and entrust him to the cult, embodying humility and moral clarity.

**Ending State:**

* Dead before Sael’s adulthood, but their moral choice shapes his destiny.
* Serve as an example of human innocence exploited by cruelty.

**Themes in Arc:**

* Innocence vs. cruelty.
* Trust and moral foresight.
* Parents’ sacrifice shaping the future.

---

## **5. Cult Leaders – Moral Guardians / Supporting Arc**

**Starting Point:**

* Persecuted moral and spiritual community, resisting Roman immorality.

**Conflict:**

* Need a symbol of hope to inspire their followers.
* Must navigate Roman threats, cult secrecy, and the ethical raising of Sael.

**Growth / Transformation:**

* Mentor Sael, instill moral and philosophical principles.
* Adapt strategy to preserve both Sael and their teachings in a hostile society.

**Ending State:**

* Survive politically by leveraging Sael’s example.
* Serve as the moral backbone of society after Sael’s death.

**Themes in Arc:**

* Persistence of ethical ideals under oppression.
* Symbolic power of morality in challenging tyranny.
* Nurturing moral heroes shapes history.

---

## **Arc Intersections / Dynamics:**

* **Sael vs. Cassian:** Moral integrity vs. political cruelty.
* **Maro vs. Self:** Envy vs. conscience.
* **Cult vs. Rome:** Moral authority vs. systemic power.
* **Parents vs. Fate:** Innocence exploited; yet they shape hope.
