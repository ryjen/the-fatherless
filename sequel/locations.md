# Locations

## Neurion Council Hall
- Description: Rebuilt civic chamber where charter debates and emergency decrees collide; high stone vaults, open skylight, and worn benches from public hearings.
- Notable moments: Charter debates, witness testimonies, and public trials of corruption.
- Visual motifs: Sunlight shafts through dust, echoing footsteps, and the tension between openness and authority.

## Lower City Commons
- Description: Crowded market lanes and gathering squares where commoners debate reform and organize relief.
- Notable moments: Street speeches, coalition meetings, and clashes with patrols.
- Visual motifs: Worn banners, chalk slogans, and improvised altars for the fallen.

## Archive Vaults
- Description: Sealed vaults beneath civic buildings where censored histories and court records are hidden.
- Notable moments: Discovery of altered decrees, recovered testimonies, and a public revelation.
- Visual motifs: Dust motes in lamplight, chained doors, and ink-stained hands.

## Pilgrim Routes
- Description: Worn roads and desert paths used by believers and traders who carry stories between cities.
- Notable moments: Quiet conversions, ambushes, and the delivery of reform petitions.
- Visual motifs: Trail markers, worn sandals, and horizon shots that emphasize distance and resolve.

## Regent Court
- Description: A provisional palace where regents barter power and draft emergency edicts.
- Notable moments: Backroom bargains, coerced oaths, and the collapse of fragile alliances.
- Visual motifs: Heavy drapes, cold marble, and guarded corridors.

## Memory Square
- Description: A public square renamed by the people, filled with memorials and quiet vigils.
- Notable moments: Annual remembrances, protest marches, and moments of collective silence.
- Visual motifs: Candles, flowers tied to railings, and worn stone steps.

## Underground Sanctuary
- Description: Hidden chambers where teachers and organizers plan and protect fugitives.
- Notable moments: Secret councils, safe-house rescues, and the swearing of mutual protection.
- Visual motifs: Narrow torchlight, low ceilings, and whispered vows.

## Border Watchtowers
- Description: Outposts along the frontier, signaling whether the city can still defend itself.
- Notable moments: Diplomatic standoffs, refugee arrivals, and warnings of incoming raids.
- Visual motifs: Smoke signals, weathered banners, and watchfires against a dark sky.
